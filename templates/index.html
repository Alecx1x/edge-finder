Closing Line Value (CLV) is the truest test of whether you're actually sharp — more reliable than win/loss, which is mostly luck in small samples. If you consistently get better odds than the line closes at, you're beating the market and profit follows long-term. Log a bet (Bet Log tab), then record what it closed at here.
Manual entry is free. “Auto” pulls the current line from the API (costs 1 request) — use it near event start.

Arbitrage = the books disagree enough that you can bet every outcome at different books and win the same no matter what. Guaranteed profit, zero prediction. The catch: arbs are small and vanish in seconds, you need money sitting at multiple books, and hammering them is the fastest way to get limited — bet modestly and not every one.

Matched betting turns a book's free bet / bonus into near-guaranteed cash: you back a pick at the book and lay it on an exchange (e.g. Betfair) so every result is covered. Enter the numbers to see your optimal lay and the locked profit. Odds are decimal (2.00 = +100). This is a calculator — it spends no API requests.

For DraftKings Pick6 / Betr-style apps. You pick player props over/under; an entry pays a fixed multiplier if all legs hit. For each leg, enter the same prop's over/under odds from a sharp book (DraftKings, Pinnacle, etc.) — we de-vig them to each leg's true hit chance, then compute the whole entry's EV. Stack legs that each beat the sharp line and the entry turns +EV.

📋 Insider Buys

The stock market's "smart money": when a company's own executives/directors buy their stock on the open market with their own money (Form 4 code P), it's a real bullish tell — they know the business. Sells and grants are noise (taxes, diversification, comp) so we score buys separately. Free SEC EDGAR data, no quota.

🔎 Smart-Wallet Discovery

Point it at a token that already pumped — it finds the wallets that bought it early, from the pool, before the run.
